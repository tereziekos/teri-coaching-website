## 6. About me

### English

I've been fascinated by the machinery of relationships since I was a child: between people, and between the parts of one person. What I mostly do now is help people untangle what's stuck.

Most of what I know came from my own inner work. I overcame conditioning from childhood that was quietly hurting me, moved from anxious to secure attachment, and found a way to be vulnerable without collapsing. I learned to communicate needs and boundaries without apologizing for them.

A few other things about me:

· I taught myself public speaking by doing stand-up comedy.
· I go to silent meditation retreats, including jhana-focused ones at Jhourney.
· I take cold baths (ask me why).
· I dance contact improvisation.
· I love Radical Honesty, including expressing anger out loud.
· I love women's circles and rituals.
· I practice tantra.
· I have a lifelong passion for trying new therapeutic approaches and experiences.
· I consider myself a rationalist, and I'm part of the effective altruism movement.

My background isn't a straight line: law at Charles University, journalism at e15 and Heroine, communications for a Czech democracy NGO, and community and mental-health work for the effective altruism movement (on grants from CEA and the EA Infrastructure Fund). I also work as a programmer on my own projects.

I believe diversity is a feature. I've collected models about the world, psychology, and relationships that I can share with you.

## 8. A note on AI

### English

Opening line (Fraunces italic, 19px, moss):

> *I'm also a programmer. That's why I can build custom AI tools for the work we do together.*

Body:

> I've trained AI agents on hundreds of books I use in coaching: IFS, Radical Honesty, NVC, tantra, mindfulness, Motivational Interviewing, CBT, and others. They give me something most coaches don't have: fast access to relevant material, tailored to what's happening in your life.
>
> Between sessions, I run your material (with your permission, your redactions, your framing) through these agents. They help me prepare for our next session: a pattern I might have missed, a frame worth trying, a specific exercise from a book I haven't re-read in a while.
>
> During sessions, when something specific surfaces and I want to bring an external lens in, I can query the agents in a few seconds instead of telling you to go read a book you won't have time for.
>
> After each session, you get a transcription and a summary that you own. A week later you can re-read what we worked on and see what still holds.
>
> I keep this visible in how I work. If you'd rather we don't use AI tools at any point, that's a clean choice, and I'll respect it.
